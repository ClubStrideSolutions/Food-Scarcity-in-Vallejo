Barriers to Food Access in Vallejo

Presenter: Ricardo

  • 1. Store Location & Transportation
    • Stores too far from residences
    • Inadequate public transportation
    • Limited carrying capacity
    "I personally think they're [stores] too far to walk, especially."
  • 2. Affordability
    • High Bay Area living costs
    • Premium prices at local markets
    • Higher costs for healthier options
    "Oftentimes we don't buy specific (healthy) foods because of its cost."
  • 3. Lack of Full-Service Grocery Stores
    • Few options in low-income areas
    • Reliance on convenience stores

Additional Challenges

Presenter: Ricardo

  • 4. Meal Adaptation
    • Changing planned meals due to missing ingredients
    • Multiple store visits required
    • Recipe abandonment
    "If I have to cook a meal, and then, man, I have to go to Food Max for this and then the Filipino place for that, do I give up that meal? Sometimes I do; it's not worth it for me to do all that."
  • 5. Poor Food Quality
    • Rotten/spoiled produce
    • Expired products
    • Sanitation concerns
    "The thing is, though, you have to eat it (the food) right away, the 99 cent store, you cannot store it (the food); it will go bad."
  • 6. Donated Foods Issues
    • Quality concerns in food assistance programs
    • Expired or substandard items

The Nutrition Gap

Presenter: Joselito

  • Limited Produce Selection:
    • 50% of stores in low-income areas have fewer than 10 types of produce
    • Only 22.7% of stores in high-income areas have this limitation
  • Organic Produce Availability:
    • Fresh organic produce in low-income areas: 4.2%
    • Fresh organic produce in high-income areas: 18.2%
  • Cleanliness Concerns:
    • Over 50% of stores in low-income areas lack "clean and sanitary environment" for produce

Beyond Statistics: A Community Story

Presenter: Jacqueline

Community Story Outline:

  • Transportation challenges
    "Riding the bus, I can only have one batch as I have a purse. Usually the bus doesn't stop right in front of you, kind of down a block. So, there's a walking distance you have to walk through like 2–3 streets away."
  • Limited shopping options
    "Both (smaller, local stores) have mainly everything but like I said less quality for a higher price."
  • Food quality concerns
    "Disappointed, if I'm wanting to make something or do something and I can't make it then I'm disappointed."
  • Emotional impact of these daily challenges

Additional Insight (March 19, 2025): According to a recent Vallejo Sun report, SolTrans is eliminating five bus routes to increase frequency on other lines. Although the changes aim for better midday service, some residents in neighborhoods like Country Club Crest now face longer than 10-minute walk times. Families with children or wheelchairs may find it even harder to reach grocery stores or return with purchases—deepening the “Missed vs. Absent” pattern in our data.

The Real-Life Consequences

Presenter: Jacqueline

Effects of Food Scarcity:

  • Day-to-day life impacts:
    • Multiple trips to different stores
    • Limited food choices
    • Time burden of food acquisition
  • Mental well-being:
    • Stress over meal planning
    • Frustration with options
    • Worry about nutrition
  • Family meal planning:
    • Improvising with available ingredients
    • Abandoning cultural recipes
    • Compromising on nutrition

"Behind every statistic is a person trying to provide healthy food for themselves and their families."
